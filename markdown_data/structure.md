Structure
=========

The website configurator provides a range of
`Structure`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} `building blocks
<../building_blocks>`{.interpreted-text role="doc"} to design your
website\'s layout, including headings, images, and text.

Below are presented two types of structure blocks:
`Banner <structure/banner>`{.interpreted-text role="ref"} and
`Masonry <structure/masonry>`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}.

::: {.note}
::: {.title}
Note
:::

To add a building block, click `Edit`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"}, select the desired building block under the
`Blocks`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} tab, and drag and drop it
onto the page. To access its settings, click it and go to the
`Customize`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} tab, where the available
options depend on the type of block selected.
:::

Banner {#structure/banner}
------

The `Banner`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} block combines a title,
text, images, and a call to action button, making it suitable for
placement at the top of a website.



### Call to action

The call to action button encourages visitors to take a specific action,
for example, consulting your shop, downloading a file, or making an
appointment.



To change the button\'s link, select it and click the
`Edit Link`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} icon. Additional
customization options are available in the
`Inline Text`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} section.



Masonry {#structure/masonry}
-------

The `Masonry`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} block offers a range of
templates that associate image and text bricks. To change the default
template, go to the `Customize`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} tab,
click `Template`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} and select one.



::: {.tip}
::: {.title}
Tip
:::

The `Masonry`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} block allows you to add
text on top of images. To do so, go to the `Customize`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"} tab, scroll to `Add Elements`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"}, and click `Text`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}.


:::
