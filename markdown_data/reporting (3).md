Reporting
=========

Odoo includes **generic** and **dynamic** reports available for all
countries, regardless of the
`localization package <../../finance/fiscal_localizations>`{.interpreted-text
role="doc"} installed:

-   `accounting/reporting/balance-sheet`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/profit-and-loss`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/executive-summary`{.interpreted-text
    role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/general-ledger`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/aged-receivable`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/aged-payable`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/cash-flow-statement`{.interpreted-text
    role="ref"}
-   `accounting/reporting/tax-report`{.interpreted-text role="ref"}

To expand the lines of a report and view its details, click the
`fa-caret-right`{.interpreted-text role="icon"}
(`right arrow`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}) on the left. Then
click the `fa-caret-down`{.interpreted-text role="icon"}
(`down arrow`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}) to the right of the
account, journal entry, payment, invoice, etc. to
`Annotate`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} and view the details.



To export reports in PDF or XLSX format, click `PDF`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"} at the top or click the
`fa-caret-down`{.interpreted-text role="icon"}
(`down arrow`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}) icon next to the
`PDF`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} button and select
`XLSX`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}.

To compare values across periods, click the
`Comparison`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} menu and select the
periods you want to compare.



Balance Sheet {#accounting/reporting/balance-sheet}
-------------

The `Balance Sheet`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} shows a snapshot
of your organization\'s assets, liabilities, and equity at a particular
date.

Profit and Loss {#accounting/reporting/profit-and-loss}
---------------

The `Profit and Loss`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} report (or
**Income Statement**) shows your company\'s net income by deducting
expenses from revenue for the reporting period.

Executive Summary {#accounting/reporting/executive-summary}
-----------------

The `Executive Summary`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} provides an
overview of all the important figures for overseeing your company\'s
performance.

It includes the following items:

-   

    `Performance`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

    :   -   

            `Gross profit margin`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   The contribution of all sales your business makes
                **minus** any direct costs needed to make those sales
                (labor, materials, etc.).

        -   

            `Net profit margin`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   The contribution of all sales made by your business
                **minus** any direct costs needed to make those sales
                *and* fixed overheads your company has (electricity,
                rent, taxes to be paid as a result of those sales,
                etc.).

        -   

            `Return on investment (per annum)`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   The ratio of the net profit to the amount of assets the
                company used to make those profits.

-   

    `Position`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

    :   -   

            `Average debtors days`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   The average number of days it takes your customers to
                (fully) pay you across all your customer invoices.

        -   

            `Average creditors days`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   The average number of days it takes you to (fully) pay
                your suppliers across all your bills.

        -   

            `Short-term cash forecast`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   How much cash is expected in or out of your business in
                the next month, i.e., the balance of your **Sales
                account** for the month **minus** the balance of your
                **Purchases account** for the month.

        -   

            `Current assets to liabilities`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}:

            :   Also referred to as the **current ratio**, this is the
                ratio of current assets (`assets
                that could be turned into cash within a year`{.interpreted-text
                role="dfn"}) to the current liabilities (`liabilities
                that will be due in the next year`{.interpreted-text
                role="dfn"}). It is typically used to measure a
                company\'s ability to service its debt.

General Ledger {#accounting/reporting/general-ledger}
--------------

The `General Ledger`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} report shows all
transactions from all accounts for a selected date range. The initial
summary report shows the totals for each account. To expand an account
and view its details, click the `fa-caret-right`{.interpreted-text
role="icon"} (`right arrow`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}) on the
left. This report is useful for reviewing each transaction that occurred
during a specific period.

Aged Receivable {#accounting/reporting/aged-receivable}
---------------

The `Aged Receivable`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} report shows
the sales invoices awaiting payment during a selected month and several
months prior.

Aged Payable {#accounting/reporting/aged-payable}
------------

The `Aged Payable`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} report displays
information on individual bills, credit notes, and overpayments you owe
and how long these have gone unpaid.

Cash Flow Statement {#accounting/reporting/cash-flow-statement}
-------------------

The `Cash Flow Statement`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} shows how
changes in balance sheet accounts and income affect cash and cash
equivalents and breaks the analysis down to operating, investing, and
financing activities.

Tax Report {#accounting/reporting/tax-report}
----------

The `Tax Report`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} shows the
`NET`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"} and `TAX`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"} amounts for all the taxes grouped by type
(`Sales`{.interpreted-text
role="guilabel"}/`Purchases`{.interpreted-text role="guilabel"}).
